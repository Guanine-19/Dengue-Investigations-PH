WHY DENGUE?

Dengue is a serious health concern locally and globally. It has been documented on every continent except Antarctica and the earliest documented case in Southeast Asia was in Manila in 1954. According to the Center for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC), 40% of the world’s population live in areas with risk of dengue. This virus is spread through the bite of an infected Aedes species mosquito and according to their data, the Philippines has frequent outbreaks or continuous transmission of this virus.

As of July 2015, The Department of Health (DOH) declared a National Dengue Alert as dengue cases continued to rise in several regions in the country (MIMAROPA, Western Visayas, Central Visayas, and Northern Mindanao). Data shows that as of August 2019, incidences of dengue almost doubled from 2018 (from 181,821 to 248,072).

This story map will show the incidences of dengue in the Philippines from 2013 to 2019 (January to August) which was retrieved from the Department of Health. It will show the years with the highest number of cases which we will link to possible causes.

According to the Philippines Atmospheric, Geophysical and Astronomical Services Administration (PAG-ASA), The climate of the Philippines is tropical and maritime. It is characterized by relatively high temperature, high humidity and abundant rainfall which are perfect climactic factors for the growth of the Aedes aegypti mosquito. According to the research conducted by Lai (2018), temperature, accumulated rainfall, and sunshine play an important role in the transmission cycles of dengue fever. As temperature increases, the Aedes aegypti mosquito displays shorter periods of development in all stages of their life cycle, which leads to increased population growth. Rainfall creates abundant outdoor breeding sources for Aedes aegypti, and the water storage containers also can serve as breeding habitats.

In relation to this, one factor that may be attributed to this doubling of incidences in the Philippines is because of global warming. Global warming is causing measurable effects such as increase in temperature and increase in annual rainfall which are both favorable to the growth of the mosquitoes aforementioned.

Most of all, what highlights this story map is that there is a need to call for action since the number of deaths in almost all regions hit their highest in the year 2018 to 2019 which either means that there is a serious problem with public health awareness or dengue prevention and control by the government.

Dengue is a serious public health concern which can lead to death. There is no specific treatment for it thus prevention and control through effective vector control measures is an utmost priority.
